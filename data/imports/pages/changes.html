In a large data reporting project like the NCCBP, data entry errors are bound to happen due to the large number of participants and the sheer size of the NCCBP workbook fields.

Under the old Excel workbook system, we had to reissue reports whenever major errors were detected. This new NCCBP website ensures that the version you see online is the most up to date report available. From now on we will correct all reported errors that are significantly affecting NCCBP Aggregate Reports.

We will report them on this page to make corrections as transparent as possible.

Method of Percentile Rank Calculation

September 2, 2011

Some of you may be aware that there are many different ways of calculating percentiles. Originally, we had implemented a new percentile calculation method on this website. Most percentile calculation methods yield the same or very similar results (within +/- 1 percent) on large data sets (e.g. the National Report). On data sets including a lower number of cases, results can differ significantly.

This is why we have decided to go back to the old NCCBP method of calculating percentiles. Please note that all percentile calculation methods have the potential to be imprecise as the number of cases decreases (e.g. in a small data set).

Affected are most regional reports and to a lesser extent the National Report.
