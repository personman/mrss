Changes in the 2016 NCCBP Data Collection

Form 2 – After some confusion in the 2015 data collection on Form 2, we have reordered the data entry fields.  We will now collect Completed AND DID NOT Transfer followed by Completed AND Transferred for each cohort.  Next, we will show you the calculated total of these two data elements – the calculated total of the two data elements will be used in the graduation rate calculations.

Form 4 – Data elements are now broken out by full-time and part-time students.  This will allow us to report the persistence rate of both the full-time and part-time students as well as the overall persistence rate.

Form 11 – We have added a section on Gateway Math.  Gateway Math is for first-level math classes required for an associate’s degree excluding college algebra, for example, statistics for business.

Form 12 and 17B – Please note that if your college does NOT offer P grades, you must enter a zero (0) in order for the benchmarks to be calculated.

 

Timeline

October
Enrollment and data collection starts. New and returning community colleges join online and are billed for the membership fee. Website sign-in information is e-mailed to subscribers. Online data collection forms are available in the member section of the website.

June
Participant institutional data are due on June 15. Data are automatically checked for missing and inconsistent information. The data verification process starts. Report that lists inconsistencies is available online for review and update.

July
Verified and updated data are due.

August
New NCCBP reports are available online and the Peer Comparison Tool is populated with most recent data.
