The American Association of University Professors was founded in 1915. Throughout its long history it has been the leading organization dedicated to protecting the academic freedom of professors.

In 1948, the AAUP's Committee on the Economic Status of the Profession published the results of its first survey of faculty salaries. In the decades since, the AAUP’s faculty salary surveys and compensation reports have been authoritative sources of information about the economic status of the profession.

In Busting the Myths: The Annual Report on the Economic Status of the Profession, 2014–15, the American Association of University Professors debunks some myths about the challenges facing higher education and highlights how faculty are a key part of the solution. The results revealed that the first consequential improvement (1.4 percent) in inflation-adjusted year-over-year salary for full-time faculty since the Great Recession.
